The promise

One patient. One record. Every facility.

Every day, health stories are lost in paper — histories retold, tests repeated, folders that never turn up. C-H-I-S replaces the folder with a single living record, made once at the door, that follows the patient through registration, triage, the ward, the pharmacy and the theatre.

No more lost files

The record is created once and cannot go missing from a shelf. Every visit adds to one continuous story.

Every action on record

Consent, observations, medicines and sign-offs are captured with a full audit trail. Defensible in care, and in review.

The whole story, at hand

Clinicians see history, medication, procedures and documents at first touch — every vital, every visit.

How it works

The record follows the patient.

One continuous record, from the first arrival to every facility after. The screens below are from the working system, captured in a demonstration environment with sample data. The pharmacy view and the national map are illustrations of features in the product.

1

Register once

A record is created at the door, with consent captured and a unique patient identifier issued. It is told once, and only once.

2

Triage and the ward

Live queues, appointments and facility activity at a glance, so staff can see the whole floor and act on what needs attention.

3

The whole story, in the notes

Chronic conditions, medication history, observations and vitals — the longitudinal record, present before the consultation begins.

4

Pharmacy and stock

Prescriptions arrive electronically, dispensing is recorded against the patient, and stock, batch and expiry are tracked so nothing runs out quietly.

5

Theatre and ICU

Bookings, anaesthetic pre-operative assessment, nursing checklists and surgeon sign-off — surgery as a governed workflow, not a whiteboard.

6

Between facilities

Referrals carry the record with them. Wherever C-H-I-S runs, the story arrives before the patient does.

Trust & privacy

Whose record is it? The patient’s.

A national health record has to answer the hard questions before anyone asks them. C-H-I-S is built on consent, auditability and South African data law.

  • Consent first. Access is granted, logged and reviewable — any patient’s access history can be produced on request.
  • Every access on the audit trail. The same mechanism that protects clinicians protects patients.
  • POPIA-conscious by design. South African data protection is an architecture requirement, not a policy afterthought.
  • Standards, not silos. HL7 FHIR resources and DHIS2 alignment mean the record can move between systems without being locked in.
